Chapter 1: The Veins of the City

Bengaluru relies on a massive, sprawling network of water supply pipelines to sustain its millions of residents.

These blue lines represent over 48,000 kilometers of the Cauvery water supply network mapped across the entire metropolitan area.

Chapter 2: The Hidden Danger

However, running parallel—and sometimes intersecting—are the city's sewage lines and storm water drains.

Spatial analysis reveals over 25,000 specific segments where the water supply network is located within 2 meters of a sewage line or manhole.

In the event of a pipe burst or heavy flooding, these glowing cyan zones carry an extremely high risk of cross-contamination.

Chapter 3: Ecological Spillover

When the system overflows, it doesn't just affect the pipes. The contamination spills into the city's natural ecology.

Notice how the primary drains (dark blue) and secondary drains (light blue) connect directly to the remaining lakes (blue polygons) and historical lost lakes (grey polygons).

Chapter 4: The Socioeconomic Impact

Who bears the brunt of this risk? By aggregating the risk points at the ward level, a stark divide emerges.

This choropleth map highlights wards in dark red that contain over 300 high-risk contamination zones. Furthermore, the overlaid pink polygons show Bengaluru's notified and non-notified slums, which frequently align with these high-risk watersheds.

Chapter 5: Citizen Audits

Citizens are tracking this crisis on the ground.

These pink markers represent localized citizen audits reporting on water contamination, foul odors, and solid waste blockages. Click on any of them to view the actual photographs submitted by volunteers.

Only through data transparency and civic action can we build a resilient Bengaluru.
